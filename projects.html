Simple, XOR based, Image Edge Detection
Course Project for Image Processing, Mentor - Prof. Sumana Gupta (July - Nov 2014)

We implemented an edge detection tool based on the ideas and algorithms proposed in a work by Diaconu et. al. This tool was then compared to the conventional Sobel, Prewitt and Canny edge detectors. We used comparisions based on visual quality of the edge images obtained. Objective measures MSE and PSNR were also calculated to demostrate the effectiveness of the algorithm. [Report][GitHub]

How do humans develop concepts?
Course Project for Cognitive Science, Mentor - Prof. Amitabha Mukherjee (July - Nov 2014)

Understanding development of concepts is a complex phenomenon. Hence, we focused on learning of concepts in the age group of 4-7 years. Modifying the existing Piaget Theory Experiments, we incorporated 'quick-learning' aspects and tested them on a group of elementary school students in campus. Source Separation using Negative-Tensor-Factorization
Course Project for Digital Signal Processing, Mentor - Prof. Rajesh Hegde (Jan - May 2014)

We modeled speech signals as tensors for an unsupervised monaural source separation algorithm. This is based on the algorithm proposed by Barker et. al. which provides a technique for Non-Negative Tensor Factorization (NTF). After implementation, we performed oracle clustering to estimate the Signal to Distortion Ratio (SDR) to quantify the effectiveness of the algorithm. [Report] [Presentation]

2D Mesh Generation
Presented in 12th Indo-European Winter Academy (Dec 2013) Mentor - Prof. G. Greiner

2D Mesh generation is an important component of computer graphics. We studied the traditional 2D-Meshing techniques. After exploration of Delaunay Traingulation and Voronoi Tesselation, we studied node insertion in existing meshes in detail. This is crucial of enhancing the existing Mesh-Structure of a given 2D image. [Presentation]

Cellular Network Optimization
Summer Project, Mentor - Prof. A.R. Harish (May - June 2012)

We designed an automation system for collecting cellular signal data (signal strength & power spectral density) using a portable workstation, Spectrum Analyzer and GPS. After conducting Drive Tests for three frequency channels of a cellular tower in IIT Kanpur (area of radius of around 1 km), we conducted a preliminary similarity analysis on the collected data. Analysis included statistical inference of the data collected for the same frequency, but on different dates and times.

Hilbert Transform on FPGA
Independent Project for Techkriti'14, Inter-College Festival, IIT Kanpur (March 2014)

We designed a synthesizable circuit on FPGA (using verilog) to compute Hilbert Transform of a 32 bit signal. The number of multiplications were reduced, with a small increase in the number of additions, to enhance the computation speed and reduce error. An error of around 0.1% was obtained for random 32 bit signals.

Tachometer
Independent Project for Techkriti'12, Inter-College Festival, IIT Kanpur (Jan 2012)

We constructed a tachometer using IR LEDs and TSOP sensors with an ATMEGA 8 microcontroller. Along with basic functionalities of a tachometer, we implemented an average filter to measure the rpm of motor along with sense of direction (clockwise or anticlockwise).
